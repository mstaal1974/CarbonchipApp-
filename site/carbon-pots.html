Circular Horticulture. Engineered Sequestration.

Carbonchip Carbon Pots are proprietary biodegradable biochar-composite nursery pots designed to replace brittle petroleum plastics. By compounding structural PLA and ductile PHA with our high-purity biochar, we have engineered a commercial pot that retains optimal tensile integrity during nursery handling, then cleanly biodegrades directly in the soil upon planting.

Material Science Matrix

Three layers. One engineered composite.

Explore the compound chemistry — click a layer of the pot, or a chip below, to reveal what each component contributes.

Layer 1 — Outer wall

Polylactic Acid (PLA)

Derived from renewable corn starch / sugarcane to provide high tensile strength and primary structural rigidity — the load-bearing skeleton that survives nursery handling and stacking.

Layer 2 — Ductility matrix

Polyhydroxyalkanoates (PHA)

Bacterial-fermented biopolymers that introduce necessary ductility and accelerate soil / marine biodegradation, so the pot flexes in handling and breaks down cleanly once planted.

Layer 3 — Proprietary filler

Proprietary Biochar Filler

High-surface-area carbonaceous material acting as a permanent carbon sink while stabilizing the composite's mechanical and thermal properties within the root zone.

The Thermal & Structural Advantage

Built for rugged commercial environments.

Unlike neat bioplastics that turn brittle or deform under extreme Australian sun, our specialized bio-composite matrix is engineered for the field. The biochar filler acts as a thermal stabilizer, offsetting the low glass transition temperature of standard polymers to protect sensitive root systems from heat stress.

Neat Bioplastic

Turns brittle or deforms under extreme sun; low glass-transition temperature exposes roots to heat stress.

Carbonchip Composite

Biochar thermal stabilizer protects the root zone; retains tensile integrity through nursery handling.

Petroleum Plastic Pot

Persists in landfill; must be stripped and disposed of at planting.

Carbon Pot

Cleanly biodegrades directly in soil upon planting — zero strip-out, permanent carbon sink.
